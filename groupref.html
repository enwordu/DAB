General

What went well?
What really kept us on top was the planning that went into the completing the report and beginning the project early. A fair bit of work was completed in our scheduled study session at the library on 30/08/2018. Due to the different types of members within a good sized team we were able to divide certain tasks depending on interest. This aided with a better final result.
What could be improved?
Although we had clear communication channels established, the level of communication in the group could have been better, especially when one could not attend a meeting. Secondly taking initiative to complete tasks would benefit the end result and workload of the team. The team fell flat early on because we didn’t follow up on progress from members nearly as much as we should of. The unspoken expectation was that we would all complete our work and contribute as required which, ultimately, was not the case.
At least one thing that was surprising.
How quickly people responded upon the threat of being moved out of the group. This realisation is quite disappointing because it involved external intervention from our tutor and lecturer. The level of expectation in terms of quality and quantity of work varies from different members. Equality is definitely hard to enforce.
At least one thing that you have learned about groups.
That although we have a common goal, our expectations vary from member to member in regards to communication, work load, quality of work and quantity. The trick is to be able to manage those expectations by clearly defining roles and what is expected.
